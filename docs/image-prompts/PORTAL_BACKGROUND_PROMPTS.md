# Dendrovia Portal Background — Image Generation Prompts

> Three iterations of increasingly refined prompts for generating the Portal page background image.
> Each draws from `SYMBOL_DRIVEN_DESIGN_SYSTEM.md`, `PILLAR_THEMATIC_SCHEMA.md`, `PILLAR_INSIGNIA_STRUCTURAL.md`, and the detailed SVG insignia vocabulary.
>
> **Target use:** Full-bleed website background at 2560x1440+, dark atmospheric, subtle enough to sit behind UI text and ornate frame cards without competing.

---

## Iteration 1 — "The Dendrite Emerges"

**Focus:** Establishing the macro composition. A single colossal dendritic structure rising from geological darkness into computational light.

```
A towering dendritic tree-structure emerges from deep archaeological darkness,
its trunk a smooth cylindrical manifold of polished computational stone (#5a8dd8)
with visible fluting grooves catching faint blue-white light. The trunk bifurcates
at a primary Y-fork junction following Murray's Law proportions — each child branch
splitting again into tertiary capillaries that dissolve into bioluminescent mist
at the canopy.

The base roots descend into stratified geological layers — twelve visible sediment
bands compressed from warm ochre (#d4a574) at the surface through amber (#8b7355)
to deep archaeological void (#1a1514) at the bottom. Circular fossil imprints with
concentric age-rings are embedded in the stone, glowing faintly blue (#7fa9f5) from
within — parsed data still alive in compressed time.

Seven golden light rays (#f8c98a) descend from the upper right at 45 degrees,
bending as they pass through the branch structure like raymarching beams through
an SDF field. Where rays intersect branch surfaces, warm convergence highlights
bloom. The ambient occlusion is implicit — deeper crevices between branches are
naturally darker from accumulated distance.

The atmosphere uses color-shift perspective, not fog: distant branches shift toward
cool desaturated blue, nearby surfaces retain warm stone tones. Floating dust motes
of amber and pale violet drift in the still air. A faint hexagonal grid pattern
(#f5e6d3 at 4% opacity) underlies the entire composition like parchment graph paper.

The background void is not black but a deep warm darkness (#0d0b0a) with subtle
radial gradients of pillar shadow-colors bleeding inward from the edges — navy
(#1e3a5f) from the upper left, deep amber (#4a3822) from the lower right,
muted violet (#4a2d5f) from the top center.

Style: Monument Valley geometric clarity meets Tron bioluminescent edges meets
archaeological cross-section illustration. Painterly but precise. No photorealism
— this is a world where mathematics is visible as beauty. The "melted plastic"
smoothness of SDF rendering is the intended aesthetic. Impossible perspective:
the structure curves in ways that suggest walking on its surface would redefine
"up" depending on your location.

Mood: Reverent, contemplative, digitally serene. The hushed air of an excavation
site at dawn combined with the crystalline precision of a rendering engine.

Palette: Deep warm void (#0d0b0a) dominant 70%, computational blue (#5a8dd8,
#8ab4f8) 15%, archaeological amber (#d4a574, #e8d7c3) 10%, accent gold (#f8c98a)
and bioluminescent blue (#7fa9f5) 5%.

Aspect ratio: 16:9, landscape. Resolution: ultra-high detail. The image should
read as a dark atmospheric background at normal viewing distance but reveal
intricate archaeological detail on closer inspection.
```

## Iteration 2 — "The Six-Pillar Convergence"

**Focus:** The six pillar identities visible as distinct material qualities within a unified dendritic landscape. More specific symbolic content.

```

bird's-eye (Falcon Mode) and intimate surface-level (Ant on a Manifold). The

its surface a gradient from polished azure (#8ab4f8) to deep navy (#1e3a5f). Nine

classical column architecture rendered through signed distance fields. The column
sits at the focal plane of a compound lens system: three concentric circular rings
(Model, View, Projection matrices) fade behind it in ghostly blueprint blue.

LEFT QUADRANT (CHRONOS zone): The dendritic branches here curve into aged parchment

showing twelve stratification bands of compressed geological epochs. Eight concentric
temporal rings emanate from a central fossil node, glowing cold bioluminescent blue

lens and precision calipers rest at the base, casting long directional shadows.

RIGHT QUADRANT (IMAGINARIUM zone): Here the branches transmute into crystalline
iridescent material. A hexagonal prism (#c6a0f6 to #e5d4ff gradient) sits at a major
fork junction, splitting incoming white light into seven ROYGBIV refraction rays that
arc outward to terminate in glowing spherical color-wells. An alembic flask shape

UPPER ZONE (ARCHITECTUS): The canopy resolves into pure geometric primitives floating

LOWER-LEFT (LUDUS): Branch surfaces here show a subtle 8x8 tactical grid texture.

LOWER-RIGHT (OPERATUS): The root system becomes industrial — interlocking gear teeth

THROUGHOUT (OCULUS): Translucent UI-like panels float at various depths — hexagonal

The atmosphere transitions through pillar color-temperatures: warm 2700K candlelight
(CHRONOS left) through neutral daylight (center) to cool 6500K bioluminescence

Style: Escher's impossible architecture meets Monument Valley's geometric clarity
meets Rez's synesthetic abstraction. Every surface has visible mathematical structure
but reads as beautiful, not clinical. The SDF "melted plastic" smoothness is
intentional — this is a world where code IS the terrain and distance fields define
reality.

Palette: 70% deep void (#0d0b0a to #1a1514), with each quadrant's pillar colors
at 20-30% local saturation. Accent highlights at full saturation only at focal
convergence points and bioluminescent nodes. The image should feel like looking
into a dark cabinet of curiosities where each shelf glows with its own light.

Composition: Golden spiral armature originating from the Y-fork junction. 60-30-10
color rule (60% dark ground, 30% mid-tone structure, 10% accent light). Depth
created through atmospheric color-shift, not opacity fog.

Aspect ratio: 16:9. Ultra-high resolution. Must remain legible as a dark background
with overlaid white/parchment text at 0.6-2.8rem sizes.
```

## Usage Notes

1. **For Midjourney/DALL-E:** Use Iteration 1 as-is for best results. Iteration 3 may exceed context limits — extract the first 2-3 paragraphs as the core prompt and append style/palette as a suffix.

2. **For Stable Diffusion (ComfyUI):** Split Iteration 3 into positive prompt (descriptive content) and use the palette hex values as ControlNet color references.

3. **For manual compositing:** Generate separate layers using zone-specific excerpts from Iteration 2, then composite in Photoshop/Figma with the correct opacity relationships.

4. **Post-processing:** The generated image should be darkened further (levels: output 0-180) and given a subtle radial vignette before use as a CSS `background-image`. Apply `background-size: cover; background-position: center 30%;` to anchor the composition at the Y-fork junction.

5. **CSS integration pattern:**
```css
.portal-background {
  background:
    radial-gradient(ellipse 60% 50% at 50% 45%, #1e3a5f30 0%, transparent 70%),
    radial-gradient(ellipse 40% 35% at 30% 70%, #4a382220 0%, transparent 60%),
    url('/backgrounds/dendrovia-portal.webp') center 30% / cover no-repeat,
    #0d0b0a;
}
```

This layers the CSS gradients (already in `page.tsx`) ON TOP of the generated image, ensuring pillar color-bleed even if the image loads slowly.
